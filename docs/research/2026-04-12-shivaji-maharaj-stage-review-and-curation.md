## Purpose

This document consolidates the multi-stage research pass for Shivaji Maharaj and turns it into a product-ready V1 curation basis for Greats of Bharatha.

The research was done in three stages:
- Stage 1: fact collection
- Stage 2: critical review and confidence audit
- Stage 3: product curation

## Core product conclusion

Shivaji Maharaj should be presented in V1 as:
- a **Hindu king**
- a builder of **Swarajya**
- a ruler shaped by forts, geography, planning, courage, and governance
- a leader whose coronation as **Chhatrapati** marked a major sovereign moment

This should be done with historical care:
- use broad stable facts over disputed micro-details
- distinguish accepted history from popular retelling where needed
- avoid sweeping communal absolutism or polemical framing
- preserve civilizational and Hindu significance without flattening the complexity of the age

## Approved framing

### Use
- Hindu ruler / Hindu king
- builder of Swarajya
- self-rule / independent rule
- crowned Chhatrapati
- dignity, protection, and responsibility
- forts, geography, and state-building
- sacred life, traditions, and institutions handled in moderate, source-aware language

### Avoid
- “all Muslims” or totalized enemy framing
- “dark age” or “all hope was lost” framing
- present-day party-political rhetoric
- myth presented as unquestioned fact
- over-cinematic violence or revenge-centered narration

## Final recommended V1 arc

Recommended scene count: **6 scenes**

### Scene 1. Shivneri, the child who would grow into a leader
- birth at Shivneri Fort
- Jijabai as a major influence
- child-safe framing of courage, duty, and beginnings

### Scene 2. Torna and Rajgad, the start of Swarajya
- Torna as an early breakthrough
- Rajgad as an early power center
- forts as instruments of planning, safety, and self-rule

### Scene 3. Pratapgad, a turning point
- major confrontation linked to Pratapgad
- strategy and terrain matter
- exact dramatic details handled carefully

### Scene 4. Purandar to Agra, pressure and escape
- setback and negotiation
- Agra as custody, pressure, and clever return
- resilience as a core leadership trait

### Scene 5. Recovery of strength
- rebuilding, persistence, regained confidence
- Sinhagad may support this in review or expansion content, but need not be a mandatory core scene

### Scene 6. Raigad, coronation as Chhatrapati
- Raigad as the sovereign climax
- coronation in 1674
- independent kingship, Swarajya, and legacy

## Map-learning conclusion

For V1, map learning should be **place and landscape learning**, not route mastery.

The child should learn a simple geographic model:
- **Konkan** = coastal belt
- **Sahyadri** = mountain wall
- **Desh / Deccan plateau** = inland highland

Best V1 map lessons:
- birth place
- first fort
- early capital
- turning-point fort
- coronation capital
- broad understanding that forts, mountains, and passes shaped strategy

Avoid in V1:
- exact historical route replay
- navigation-like path mastery
- overprecise campaign reconstruction

## Timeline milestones

Use order first, precision second.

Preferred chronology for ages 8 to 11:
- birth at Shivneri
- early forts
- Pratapgad turning point
- Purandar pressure
- Agra escape
- recovery / comeback
- Raigad coronation

Supporting date anchors for older children in-band:
- 1630 (commemorative birth date commonly used)
- 1646/1647 Torna
- 1659 Pratapgad
- 1665 Purandar
- 1666 Agra
- 1674 Raigad coronation
