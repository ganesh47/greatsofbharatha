# Shivaji Maharaj first-character product spec

- Status: Draft
- Linked issue: #3
- Milestone: Foundation
- Owner: @ganesh47
- Last updated: 2026-04-12

## Problem

The app needs a first flagship character whose journey can prove the product loop for story, place, time, memory, and replay.

## Decision

The first flagship character will be **Shivaji Maharaj**.

## Why Shivaji Maharaj

- strong biographical arc
- highly memorable place-based history
- forts and terrain make map learning meaningful
- event sequencing is naturally teachable
- high emotional and cultural resonance
- supports future expansion into Maratha context, western India geography, and related figures

## Product goals

- help a child remember Shivaji Maharaj through story, map, timeline, and recall
- teach key forts, regions, and events
- make the child comfortable locating major places on the map
- support repeat review through low-friction memory loops

## Non-goals

- full scholarly treatment of the entire Maratha period in V1
- detailed military simulation
- turn-by-turn navigation product behavior
- overwhelming children with long reading passages

## Target age band

Primary:
- ages 8 to 11

Support:
- simpler assistive mode for younger children

## Experience structure

### 1. Intro hook
A child-friendly opening that frames Shivaji Maharaj as a leader shaped by courage, strategy, geography, and vision.

### 2. Story journey
Five to seven scenes, for example:
- early life and influences
- first forts and mountain strategy
- key turning points
- expansion and leadership
- coronation and legacy

### 3. Map layer
For each scene, connect to place anchors such as:
- Shivneri
- Torna
- Rajgad
- Raigad
- Pratapgad
- Purandar
- Sinhagad

### 4. Timeline layer
Teach event order and broad chronology using:
- before/after for younger children
- dated milestones for older children within target band

### 5. Memory hooks
Possible hooks:
- fort symbol system
- short phrase memory cues
- 3-word summaries per chapter

### 6. Recall loop
Question types:
- pin the fort
- what happened next
- place this event on the timeline
- match place to event
- choose the right region/route

### 7. Review loop
Spaced review after completion:
- next day
- three days later
- one week later

## Content principles

- respectful and historically careful tone
- clear separation where legend and history differ
- age-appropriate complexity
- no triumphalist or dehumanizing framing
- focus on understanding, not slogan repetition

## Success criteria

A child should be able to:
- name Shivaji Maharaj
- identify 3 to 5 key places connected to him
- sequence major events at a simple level
- explain in simple language why he mattered
- return later and recall core facts with minimal cues

## Open questions

- which exact 5 to 7 scenes should define V1?
- which forts should be mandatory in the first release?
- should route-learning be introduced in the first character flow or shortly after?
