## Purpose

Define the canonical V1 lesson flow for Shivaji Maharaj in Greats of Bharatha.

This spec combines:
- Stage 1 historical fact collection
- Stage 2 critical review and framing audit
- Stage 3 product curation
- deep child-experience research for ages 8 to 11
- premium iPhone capability and sensor strategy

The target experience should feel:
- story-first
- map-rich
- timeline-aware
- premium but calm
- child-safe
- compatible across modern iPhones without losing core learning value

## Product thesis

The Shivaji Maharaj lesson should feel like:

**story → spatial discovery → strategic reconstruction → short recall → meaningful reward**

The child should finish with:
- a clear sense of Shivaji Maharaj’s journey
- memory of key places
- a simple time sequence
- understanding of why he mattered
- emotional connection without historical distortion

## Audience

Primary age band:
- **8 to 11**

Supporting assumptions:
- low to moderate reading load
- strong visual learning
- better understanding through concrete places, symbols, and sequence
- no dependence on fast reflexes or long text passages

## Core design principles

- one main idea per scene
- each scene answers:
  - where are we?
  - what happened?
  - why does it matter?
- touch-first interaction
- story and recall are interleaved
- progress always visible
- no punitive fail states
- polish over noise
- premium sensor use is enhancement, never the only path

## Canonical scene count

Use exactly **6 scenes**.

Do not expand the core lesson beyond 6 scenes in V1.

## Canonical lesson loop

Each scene should follow this pattern:
1. **Hook**
2. **Story beat**
3. **Map or timeline action**
4. **Short recall**
5. **Meaningful reward**

Typical scene duration target:
- 90 seconds to 150 seconds

Full first-play target:
- 10 to 15 minutes

## Canonical scenes

### Scene 1. Shivneri, the child who would grow into a leader

**Narrative objective**
Introduce Shivaji Maharaj as a real child shaped by place, values, and Jijabai’s guidance.

**Key fact**
Shivaji Maharaj was born at Shivneri Fort. His mother Jijabai is a major influence in how this story is remembered and taught.

**Map anchor**
- primary: Shivneri Fort
- regional context: hill-fort country near Junnar, north of Pune

**Timeline action**
Place first marker:
- `Born at Shivneri`

**Recall prompt**
- Which fort is Shivaji Maharaj’s birth place?

**Child-safe wording pattern**
“At Shivneri Fort, a child named Shivaji began his journey. Jijabai helped teach him courage, duty, and care for his people.”

**Recommended interaction**
- tap meaningful symbols in a calm opening tableau
- reveal 3 anchors: fort, mother, future kingdom

**Reward**
- unlock `Birth Fort` card in the Royal Chronicle

**Sensory cues**
- morning fort ambience
- light wind over stone walls
- soft bell tone
- gentle reveal haptic

**Guardrails**
- birth date should be framed carefully if shown
- safest wording: commonly commemorated as 1630

---

### Scene 2. Torna and Rajgad, the start of Swarajya

**Narrative objective**
Show the move from childhood to action, and connect early fort capture with state-building.

**Key fact**
Torna was an early breakthrough. Rajgad became an important early capital or power center.

**Map anchor**
- primary: Torna
- secondary: Rajgad

**Timeline action**
Move to:
- `Early forts`

Optional date badge:
- `c. 1646 to 1654`

**Recall prompt**
- Which fort came first, Torna or Raigad?
- Which fort became an early capital?

**Child-safe wording pattern**
“Shivaji Maharaj began building Swarajya by taking strong forts and planning carefully from the mountains.”

**Recommended interaction**
- map zoom from Shivneri to Torna to Rajgad
- simple fort-building or fort-choice interaction
- optional placement of gate/watch/water/storage in a toy-like fort system

**Reward**
- unlock `First Big Fort` card
- add fort badge to Chronicle

**Sensory cues**
- rising drums
- flag unfurl animation
- snapping stone/build sounds
- light success haptic on correct placement

**Guardrails**
- avoid brittle certainty on exact capture method
- keep focus on initiative and planning, not trickery detail

---

### Scene 3. Pratapgad, a turning point

**Narrative objective**
Teach that terrain, planning, and nerve changed the balance.

**Key fact**
The confrontation near Pratapgad in 1659 became a major turning point in Shivaji Maharaj’s rise.

**Map anchor**
- primary: Pratapgad
- context: steep hill country and passes

**Timeline action**
Move to:
- `Pratapgad changes everything`

Optional date badge:
- `1659`

**Recall prompt**
- At which fort did a major turning point happen?

**Child-safe wording pattern**
“At Pratapgad, Shivaji Maharaj used planning, local terrain, and courage in a dangerous moment.”

**Recommended interaction**
- read-signals or preparation mini-game
- identify safe clues, terrain clues, and strategic choices

**Reward**
- unlock `Turning Point` card
- reveal fort-emblem fragment

**Sensory cues**
- forest mist
- lookout drum pulse
- one stronger haptic for key reveal
- tense but non-graphic sound design

**Guardrails**
- do not depict gore
- do not present disputed micro-details as certainty
- emphasize turning point, preparedness, and terrain

---

### Scene 4. Purandar to Agra, pressure and escape

**Narrative objective**
Teach setback, hard compromise, and clever survival.

**Key fact**
Purandar brought major pressure and a difficult agreement. Agra brought humiliation, close watch, and eventual escape and return.

**Map anchor**
- primary: Purandar
- secondary: Agra

**Timeline action**
Move through paired beats:
- `Pressure at Purandar`
- `Agra and return`

Optional date badges:
- `1665`
- `1666`

**Recall prompt**
- Which happened first, Purandar or Agra?
- In which northern city was Shivaji held under watch?

**Child-safe wording pattern**
“Sometimes leaders face hard pressure. Shivaji Maharaj had to make a difficult agreement, and later found a clever way to return home.”

**Recommended interaction**
- map stretch from Maharashtra to north India
- sequence-planning puzzle for the return/escape logic

**Reward**
- unlock `Escape and Return` card
- add northern-route memory ribbon to Chronicle

**Sensory cues**
- quiet court ambience
- dimmed hall visual language
- seal-stamp sounds on correct sequence
- restrained suspense pulse

**Guardrails**
- avoid over-certainty on basket folklore details
- keep the emphasis on resilience, patience, and clever planning

---

### Scene 5. Recovery of strength

**Narrative objective**
Show resilience, rebuilding, and regained confidence after setbacks.

**Key fact**
After Agra, Shivaji Maharaj rebuilt strength, reorganized, and returned to growth.

**Map anchor**
- primary: Rajgad as comeback anchor
- optional review mention: later regained forts, including Sinhagad as expansion content

**Timeline action**
Move to:
- `Comeback and rebuilding`

**Recall prompt**
- What did Shivaji do after returning from Agra?

**Child-safe wording pattern**
“After a difficult time, Shivaji Maharaj did not give up. He rebuilt, reorganized, and grew stronger again.”

**Recommended interaction**
- relight the map network
- reconnect forts or restore Chronicle fragments
- short reconstruction task rather than a battle scene

**Reward**
- unlock `Comeback` card
- restore another royal seal fragment

**Sensory cues**
- sunrise-after-storm palette
- messenger/hoof audio hints
- warm rebuild chime
- progressive map relighting

**Guardrails**
- do not overload this scene with extra battles
- keep focus on resilience and recovery

---

### Scene 6. Raigad, coronation as Chhatrapati

**Narrative objective**
Deliver the sovereign climax and explain why Shivaji Maharaj mattered.

**Key fact**
At Raigad in 1674, Shivaji Maharaj was crowned Chhatrapati, marking a major moment of self-rule and sovereign kingship.

**Map anchor**
- primary: Raigad

**Timeline action**
Place final marker:
- `Raigad coronation`

Optional date badge:
- `1674`

**Recall prompt**
- At which fort was Shivaji Maharaj crowned Chhatrapati?
- Why is Raigad important?

**Child-safe wording pattern**
“At Raigad, Shivaji Maharaj was crowned Chhatrapati. This marked the rise of self-rule, or Swarajya.”

**Recommended interaction**
- ceremonial assembly
- place earned symbols into final tableau
- complete the Royal Chronicle page

**Reward**
- unlock `Chhatrapati Crown` ending card
- complete first Chronicle set
- unlock mastery recap

**Sensory cues**
- ceremonial drums and conch
- bright saffron-gold finish
- layered but restrained celebration
- strong milestone haptic, used only here

**Guardrails**
- explain political and sovereign significance, not only ritual spectacle
- keep the scene proud, not triumphalist against communities

## Approved framing language

### Use
- Hindu king
- builder of Swarajya
- self-rule / independent rule
- crowned Chhatrapati
- dignity, protection, responsibility
- planning, forts, geography, governance

### Use carefully
- sacred life, traditions, and institutions
- difficult times / unsettled times
- pressure, uncertainty, harsh rule in some contexts

### Avoid
- blanket “all Muslims” or totalized enemy framing
- “dark age” language
- revenge-heavy narration
- myth as certainty
- present-day party-political rhetoric

## Reward system

Use the **Royal Chronicle** as the primary reward structure.

Each scene grants:
- 1 story card
- 1 artifact or emblem fragment
- 1 meaning or leadership badge

Suggested mastery states:
- Witnessed
- Understood
- Observed Closely

Do not use anxiety-heavy stars or failure grades in the core flow.

## Interaction rules for ages 8 to 11

- large tap targets
- generous drag zones
- magnetic snapping on map and timeline placement
- no hidden gestures
- one primary CTA per screen
- narration available and interruptible
- low reading load
- wrong answers give quick logic, not punishment
- if no interaction occurs for 20 to 30 seconds, reduce passive scene length or surface a hint

## Premium iPhone capability strategy

### Core on all supported iPhones
These must carry the full lesson value:
- touch-first storytelling
- high-quality narration and captions
- map interactions
- timeline interactions
- light haptics
- optional gentle motion/parallax
- offline lesson availability
- accessibility-first behavior

### Enhanced on Pro-class devices
These can elevate the experience without changing the content:
- LiDAR-backed AR fort or room-scale mini-scene moments
- richer room-aware 3D fort placement
- smoother high-frame transitions
- more cinematic sensor-assisted reveals
- enhanced photo/creation moments

### Optional on capable devices
- pronunciation practice via short speech flows
- collectible AR artifacts
- widget-powered progress and re-entry surfaces

### Must not be required
- long mandatory AR sessions
- UWB-dependent mechanics
- precise gyro-only gameplay
- always-on speech or mic usage
- battery-heavy spectacle loops

## Sensor and feedback guidance by category

### Haptics
Use for:
- selection confirmation
- discovery
- milestone release
- ceremonial moments

Avoid:
- repetitive or long-duration buzzing

### Motion / gyroscope
Use for:
- tilt to inspect
- gentle parallax
- brief spyglass or fort-view moments

Always provide touch fallback.

### Camera
Use for:
- short optional scan/reveal tasks
- capture a memory postcard
- optional creative mode

Never require long camera sessions to progress.

### LiDAR / AR
Use on Pro-class devices for:
- short wow moments, 30 to 90 seconds
- fort placement and walk-around viewing
- room-aware depth and occlusion

Fallback on non-Pro:
- plane-based AR or touch-controlled 3D diorama

### Audio
Use:
- premium voice acting
- short earcons
- clean narration-first mixes

Avoid:
- dense soundtrack competing with comprehension

## Accessibility and safety

- captions and transcripts for all narration
- VoiceOver support across scenes, maps, buttons, rewards
- reduced motion mode disables parallax and dramatic transitions
- no color-only encoding for meaning
- no location requirement in #6
- no social comparison pressure
- no school-hour spam notifications as part of lesson logic

## End-of-arc mastery round

At lesson end, include a short mixed recap:
- place scenes in order
- match place to event
- pin 3 to 5 places on the map
- answer: `Why does Shivaji Maharaj matter?`

Suggested final mastery sentence:
“Shivaji Maharaj was a Hindu king who built Swarajya and was crowned Chhatrapati at Raigad.”

## Dependencies and handoff

### This spec drives #7
#7 should use the core 5 place set from this lesson.

### This spec constrains #8
#8 must remain a non-blocking, educational orientation layer, not live navigation.

## Acceptance criteria

The V1 lesson flow is correct when:
- the 6 canonical scenes are present
- each scene has one dominant story beat and map anchor
- order is taught more strongly than date memorization
- Raigad coronation is the climax
- touch-first interaction works fully without premium sensors
- premium sensors enhance but do not gate comprehension
- the flow feels calm, polished, and premium for children
- the final recap proves memory of people, places, sequence, and meaning
